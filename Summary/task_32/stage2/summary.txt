The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (EMRIP) from Eastern European States in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate and requirements of the position. These features include:

1. **Language Proficiency**: All successful candidates demonstrate a high level of proficiency in English and Russian, which are crucial for effective communication and collaboration within the international arena of the UNHRC. This proficiency ensures that candidates can fully engage in discussions, contribute to reports, and interact with various stakeholders without language barriers.

2. **Relevant Background and Experience**: Successful candidates possess a mix of backgrounds that include experience in NGOs, IGOs, and government sectors. This diverse experience is valuable as it provides a comprehensive understanding of the issues related to indigenous rights from various perspectives, including advocacy, international cooperation, and policy-making.

3. **Current Engagement in Relevant Fields**: Candidates currently involved in NGO and law-related work are preferred. This indicates their active participation and commitment to human rights issues, particularly in the context of indigenous peoples' rights. Their ongoing engagement ensures that they are up-to-date with current challenges and developments in this field.

4. **Age Diversity**: While age is not a definitive factor, the presence of candidates across different age groups (from mid-career to more experienced professionals) contributes to a balanced perspective and ensures a mix of both seasoned insights and fresh ideas.

These key features serve as clear guidelines for the selection process, emphasizing the need for candidates who are well-equipped linguistically, experienced in relevant fields, actively engaged in current human rights work, and capable of bringing diverse perspectives to the table.
