The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (EMRIP) in the UNHRC exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features provide a clear guideline for future candidate selection:

Some also demonstrate proficiency in Arabic and other languages relevant to the African context. This linguistic diversity ensures effective communication and understanding across different indigenous communities and stakeholders.

2. **Diverse Professional Backgrounds**: Candidates with a mix of legal, NGO, government, and IGO backgrounds are favored. This diversity of experience allows for a comprehensive understanding and approach to the complex issues surrounding indigenous rights. Legal expertise is particularly valued, as it ensures a solid foundation in human rights law and advocacy.

3. **Relevant Current Employment**: Many successful candidates are currently engaged in roles related to NGOs, governments, or international organizations. This indicates their active involvement and commitment to human rights and indigenous issues, providing them with practical experience and insights that are valuable to the EMRIP.

4. **Geographical Representation**: Successful candidates come from various African countries, ensuring a broad representation of the continent's diverse indigenous populations. This geographical diversity is crucial for addressing the specific needs and challenges faced by indigenous peoples across different regions.

5. **Age Diversity**: While age is not a definitive factor, the range of ages among successful candidates suggests a balance between seasoned professionals with extensive experience and those with fresh perspectives and innovative approaches.

In summary, the selection of candidates for EMRIP in the UNHRC emphasizes linguistic proficiency, diverse professional backgrounds, active engagement in relevant fields, geographical representation, and a balance of experience levels. These criteria ensure that the selected experts are well-equipped to address the multifaceted challenges of indigenous rights and contribute effectively to the mandate's objectives.
