The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (EMRIP) from Central and Eastern Europe, the Russian Federation, Central Asia, and Transcaucasia, as shortlisted by the United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC), exhibit several key features that align with the mandate and requirements of the position. These features can serve as clear guidelines for future candidate selection processes:

1. **Multilingual Proficiency**: Successful candidates demonstrate a high level of proficiency in both English and Russian. This linguistic capability is crucial for effective communication and collaboration within the international framework of the UNHRC, where English and Russian are the primary working languages.

2. **Diverse Professional Backgrounds**: The candidates possess a mix of professional backgrounds that include legal, NGO, government, and, in some cases, academic and international governmental organization (IGO) experience. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding and approach to the complex issues surrounding indigenous rights.

3. **Relevant Current Employment**: All shortlisted candidates are currently engaged in roles that are directly related to NGOs and law, reflecting their active involvement and commitment to human rights and legal frameworks. This practical experience is vital for addressing the specific needs and challenges faced by indigenous communities.

4. **Alignment with Mandate**: The candidates' backgrounds and current roles align well with the mandate of EMRIP, which involves providing expertise, recommendations, and support for the promotion and protection of the rights of indigenous peoples. Their experiences in legal advocacy, policy development, and community engagement are particularly relevant.

5. **International Exposure**: Candidates with experience in IGOs or international settings demonstrate a broader perspective and familiarity with global human rights mechanisms, which is beneficial for representing the interests of indigenous peoples at the international level.

These key features underscore the importance of language skills, professional diversity, current relevance, and alignment with the mandate of EMRIP in the selection of candidates for positions within the UNHRC. These criteria ensure that selected experts are well-equipped to contribute effectively to the advancement of indigenous rights on a global stage.
