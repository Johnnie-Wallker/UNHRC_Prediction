5. **Legal Tradition**: Candidates with a strong background in international law, particularly those educated in diverse legal traditions and jurisdictions, are more likely to be successful. This includes those with education and experience in both common law and civil law systems, reflecting the need for a broad understanding of legal frameworks across different regions.

6. **Location of Education**: Candidates who have pursued education in both OECD countries and global south regions are well-represented. This indicates that a diverse educational background, encompassing different global perspectives, is advantageous. It suggests that the UNHRC values candidates who have been exposed to a variety of educational environments and legal systems.

In conclusion, the selection process for the Special Rapporteur position emphasizes a balance of experience, linguistic proficiency, gender equality, and regional diversity. Candidates with a strong international legal background and a diverse educational experience are particularly favored. This approach ensures that the selected candidate can effectively address the complex and multifaceted issues related to unilateral coercive measures and human rights.
