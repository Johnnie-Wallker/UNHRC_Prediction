The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Right to Development (EMRTD) from Asia-Pacific States in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key characteristics that align with the mandate's requirements. These features include:

1. **Diverse Backgrounds**: Successful candidates possess a blend of legal, academic, and sometimes NGO or government backgrounds. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding and approach to the right to development, encompassing both theoretical and practical perspectives.

2. **Language Proficiency**: While English proficiency is crucial, candidates also demonstrate additional language skills, particularly Spanish, which is beneficial for engaging with a broader range of stakeholders within the UN framework.

3. **Relevant Experience**: Candidates have current or recent experience in academic roles, indicating a strong foundation in research, analysis, and the dissemination of knowledge, which is essential for contributing to the expert mechanism's objectives.

4. **Age and Maturity**: The age range of successful candidates, spanning from the late 30s to the mid-50s, suggests a preference for individuals with a balance of youthful energy and seasoned experience, capable of bringing both innovative ideas and mature judgment to the role.

5. **National Representation**: Successful candidates represent key countries within the Asia-Pacific region, ensuring geographical and cultural diversity, which is vital for addressing the varied development challenges across the region.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the importance of a well-rounded profile that combines expertise, experience, and linguistic capabilities to effectively serve the mandate of the EMRTD within the UNHRC.
