The summary of key features for successful candidates for the Special Rapporteur on trafficking in persons, especially women and children, in the UN Human Rights Council (UNHRC) reveals several trends and characteristics that are indicative of the selection criteria:

1. **Age Range**: The successful candidates tend to fall within a mature age range, typically between 50 to 60 years old. This suggests that experience and a seasoned career trajectory are highly valued for this role.

2. **Language Abilities**: Fluency in major international languages, particularly English and French, is a significant advantage. Most successful candidates exhibit high proficiency in English, with many also being proficient in French. This aligns with the multilingual requirements of the UN and the need for effective communication across different regions.

3. **Gender Balance**: The selection process appears to be balanced in terms of gender, with both male and female candidates being shortlisted. This indicates a commitment to gender equality in the selection for this role.

4. **Nationality and Regional Representation**: Successful candidates come from diverse nationalities and regions, including Europe, South America, North Africa, and the Middle East. This diversity reflects the global nature of the mandate and the need for a broad understanding of trafficking issues across different cultural and legal contexts.

5. **Legal Tradition**: Candidates with a strong legal background, particularly in international law, criminal law, and human rights law, are more likely to be successful. This is evident from the educational and professional backgrounds of the candidates, which often include specialized training and experience in these areas.

6. **Location of Education**: Education from diverse locations, including both OECD countries and the global south, is seen as beneficial. This diversity in educational backgrounds suggests that the UNHRC values a broad perspective and understanding of global issues related to trafficking in persons.

In conclusion, the selection process for the Special Rapporteur on trafficking in persons emphasizes candidates who are mature, multilingual, and have a strong legal and human rights background. The selection also values diversity in terms of gender, nationality, and educational experiences, reflecting the global and multifaceted nature of the mandate.
