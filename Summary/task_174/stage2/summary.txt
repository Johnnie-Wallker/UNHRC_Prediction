The successful candidates for the position of Special Rapporteur on the situation of human rights in Myanmar, as discussed in the UNHRC meeting, exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features include:

1. **Diverse Backgrounds**: Successful candidates possess a blend of backgrounds, including legal, academic, and NGO experiences. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of human rights issues and the ability to address them from multiple perspectives.

2. **Strong English Proficiency**: All successful candidates demonstrate high proficiency in English, which is crucial for effective communication and negotiation within the international arena.

3. **Relevant Current Job Experience**: Candidates currently engaged in roles related to law, international governmental organizations (IGO), academia, and NGOs are preferred. This reflects their active involvement and commitment to human rights issues.

4. **Age Diversity**: The age range of successful candidates varies, indicating that experience and maturity are valued alongside the energy and fresh perspectives that younger candidates can bring.

5. **International Exposure**: Candidates with backgrounds in IGOs and government demonstrate a strong international exposure, which is essential for navigating the complexities of global human rights issues.

6. **Language Skills Beyond English**: While English proficiency is a must, candidates with additional language skills, such as French, are considered advantageous, enhancing their ability to communicate with a broader range of stakeholders.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the need for candidates who are well-rounded, experienced, and capable of effectively addressing the human rights challenges in Myanmar.
