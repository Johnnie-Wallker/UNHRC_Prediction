The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Right to Development (EMRTD) from African States in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key qualities that align with the mandate's requirements. These qualities include:

1. **Legal Background**: All successful candidates possess a strong legal background, indicating their expertise in human rights law and related fields. This is crucial for addressing and advising on legal aspects of the right to development.

2. **Academic Background**: The candidates demonstrate a robust academic background, reflecting their deep understanding and research capabilities in development issues and human rights. This academic grounding is essential for contributing to scholarly discussions and providing evidence-based recommendations.

3. **Proficiency in English**: Each candidate has a high level of English proficiency, which is vital for effective communication and participation in international forums where English is the primary working language.

4. **Relevant Professional Experience**: The successful candidates have current professional experiences that are closely related to law and development. This includes roles in academia, government, and international governmental organizations, ensuring they have practical experience in implementing and advocating for the right to development.

5. **Diverse Nationalities**: The inclusion of candidates from different African countries and even dual citizenship (where applicable) reflects a commitment to regional representation and diversity, enhancing the mechanism's ability to address issues from various national perspectives.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the importance of legal expertise, academic rigor, language proficiency, relevant professional experience, and regional representation in the selection of members for the EMRTD.
