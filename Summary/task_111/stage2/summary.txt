The successful candidates for the position of Special Rapporteur on the rights of persons with disabilities in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features include:

1. **Multilingual Proficiency**: Successful candidates demonstrate proficiency in multiple languages, particularly English and either Arabic or French. This linguistic diversity is crucial for effectively communicating and advocating for the rights of persons with disabilities across different regions and linguistic communities.

2. **Diverse Professional Backgrounds**: The candidates possess a mix of legal, academic, government, IGO (Intergovernmental Organization), NGO (Non-Governmental Organization), and company backgrounds. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of various perspectives and approaches to disability rights, from policy-making to grassroots advocacy.

3. **Relevant Current Job Experience**: Successful candidates are currently engaged in roles that are directly or indirectly related to disability rights, such as working in NGOs, IGOs, governments, or law-related positions. This experience ensures that they are actively involved in the field and are up-to-date with current challenges and advancements in disability rights.

4. **Age Diversity**: The shortlisted candidates range in age, reflecting a balance between seasoned professionals with extensive experience and younger candidates with fresh perspectives and energy. This age diversity ensures a dynamic and inclusive approach to the mandate.

5. **International and Cross-Cultural Experience**: Candidates with dual citizenship or experience working in multiple countries are preferred. This international exposure enhances their ability to navigate and address global issues related to disability rights effectively.

In summary, the selection process for the Special Rapporteur on the rights of persons with disabilities emphasizes candidates who are multilingual, have diverse and relevant professional backgrounds, are currently active in the field, represent a range of ages, and possess international and cross-cultural experience. These criteria ensure that the selected candidate is well-equipped to fulfill the mandate effectively and advocate for the rights of persons with disabilities globally.
