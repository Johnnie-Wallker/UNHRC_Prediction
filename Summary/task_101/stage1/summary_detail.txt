4. **Nationality and Regional Representation**: The successful candidates come from diverse regions, including Europe, North America, and Asia. This includes those educated in various legal traditions, such as common law and civil law systems. 6. **Location of Education**: The diversity in the location of education, spanning both OECD countries and the global south, suggests that the UNHRC values a broad educational foundation. Candidates who have studied in multiple regions or have a mix of international and local educational experiences are favored, indicating a preference for a global outlook and understanding of local contexts.

In conclusion, the selection of candidates for the Special Rapporteur on the rights of indigenous peoples emphasizes a blend of experience, language proficiency, gender balance, regional diversity, legal expertise, and educational breadth.
