The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (EMRIP) member from the Arctic, as observed in the selection process for the UN Human Rights Council, exhibit several key features that align with the mandate and requirements of the position. These features include:

1. **Strong Legal Background**: All successful candidates possess a robust legal background, which is crucial for understanding and addressing the complex legal issues surrounding indigenous rights. This background equips them to contribute effectively to the formulation and implementation of policies and frameworks that protect indigenous peoples' rights.

2. **Diverse Professional Experience**: The candidates have demonstrated a variety of professional experiences, including academic, governmental, intergovernmental organization (IGO), and non-governmental organization (NGO) backgrounds. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of the issues from multiple perspectives and enhances their ability to advocate for and implement effective solutions.

3. **Academic and Research Orientation**: A significant number of successful candidates are currently engaged in academic or research-related roles. This indicates a strong emphasis on evidence-based approaches and a commitment to advancing knowledge in the field of indigenous rights.

4. **Language Proficiency**: While proficiency in English is a common trait among the successful candidates, fluency in other relevant languages, particularly those spoken in the Arctic region, is also considered advantageous. This proficiency facilitates effective communication and collaboration with indigenous communities and other stakeholders.

5. **Relevant Current Engagement**: Successful candidates are actively involved in roles that are directly or indirectly related to indigenous rights, ensuring that they are up-to-date with current issues and developments in the field.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the importance of legal expertise, diverse professional backgrounds, academic engagement, language skills, and active involvement in relevant areas. These criteria ensure that candidates are well-equipped to fulfill the responsibilities of the EMRIP role effectively and contribute meaningfully to the protection and promotion of indigenous peoples' rights in the Arctic region.
