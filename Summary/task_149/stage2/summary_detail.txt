5. **Legal Tradition and Education**: Candidates' legal traditions vary, with education backgrounds from both local and international institutions. This indicates that a broad legal education, whether within Africa or internationally, is considered valuable. The diversity in educational backgrounds, including universities in OECD countries and the global south, suggests that a varied educational exposure is beneficial.

6. **Location of Education**: The location of education is diverse, with candidates having studied in various countries including South Africa, the USA, the UK, and Kenya. This diversity in educational settings suggests that international exposure and understanding of different legal systems are advantageous.

In conclusion, the selection process for the Working Group emphasizes a balance of age, language proficiency, gender, and regional representation, alongside a broad and diverse legal education.
