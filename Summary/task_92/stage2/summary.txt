The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (EMRIP) member from the Arctic, as observed in the UNHRC meetings, exhibit several key features that align with the mandate and requirements of the position. These features are crucial for guiding future candidate selection processes:

1. **Multifaceted Background**: Successful candidates possess a diverse background that includes legal expertise, complemented by experience in academic, NGO, and government sectors. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding and approach to indigenous rights issues.

2. **Strong Legal and Academic Foundations**: A robust legal background is essential, as it provides the necessary framework for addressing and advocating for indigenous rights within a legal context. Additionally, academic experience contributes to a deeper understanding of indigenous issues through research and education.

3. **Engagement with Multiple Sectors**: Candidates who have worked in various sectors such as NGOs, international governmental organizations (IGOs), and government bodies demonstrate a broad perspective and practical experience in implementing and influencing policies related to indigenous peoples.

4. **Language Proficiency**: While English proficiency is highly valued, candidates with additional language skills, particularly those relevant to the Arctic region, are advantageous. This proficiency facilitates effective communication and collaboration with indigenous communities and other stakeholders.

5. **Relevant Current Employment**: Candidates currently engaged in roles that intersect law, academia, and advocacy are seen as actively maintaining their expertise and relevance in the field of indigenous rights.

These key features collectively ensure that candidates are well-equipped to contribute effectively to the work of EMRIP, advocating for and protecting the rights of indigenous peoples in the Arctic region.
