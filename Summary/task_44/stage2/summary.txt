The successful candidates for the position of Special Rapporteur on torture and other cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit a robust combination of legal expertise, international experience, and a strong background in human rights advocacy. 3. **International Experience**: Successful candidates have experience working in or with international organizations (IGOs), which is crucial for understanding and addressing global human rights issues. This experience also facilitates effective communication and collaboration with other UN bodies and member states.

4. **Language Proficiency**: High proficiency in English is a common trait among the successful candidates, reflecting the necessity of strong communication skills in the primary working language of the UN. Some candidates also possess additional language skills, such as French and Spanish, which enhance their ability to engage with a broader international audience.

5. **Age and Experience**: The candidates range in age, indicating a mix of seasoned professionals and those with extensive experience in the field. This blend ensures a balance between deep knowledge and fresh perspectives on human rights issues.

6. **Current Relevance**: The successful candidates are currently engaged in roles that are directly or indirectly related to human rights, ensuring that they are actively involved and up-to-date with contemporary human rights challenges and developments.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the importance of legal acumen, international exposure, diverse professional experiences, and effective communication skills in candidates for the Special Rapporteur position.
