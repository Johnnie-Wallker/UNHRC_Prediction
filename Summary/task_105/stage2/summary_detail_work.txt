The shortlisted candidates for the Special Rapporteur on the implications for human rights of the environmentally sound management and disposal of hazardous substances and wastes exhibit a range of expertise and experience relevant to the mandate. Both candidates have demonstrated significant academic and practical contributions in the fields of environmental law and human rights.

One candidate, a seasoned professor from a European university, has extensive experience in European and international environmental law, including roles as a senior fellow and head of environmental governance programs. This candidate has also served in various advisory and legal capacities within European institutions, providing a robust background in policy development and legal frameworks.

The other candidate, with a background in environmental law and policy from North America, has a strong record of academic teaching and research, complemented by practical experience as an environmental lawyer and consultant. This candidate has led significant environmental initiatives at the city level and has advised national governments on sustainability issues, showcasing a blend of theoretical knowledge and practical application.

Both candidates possess high levels of proficiency in English and French, which are crucial for effective communication and collaboration within the United Nations framework. Their diverse experiences in both academic and practical settings make them well-suited to address the complex and interdisciplinary nature of the mandate.
