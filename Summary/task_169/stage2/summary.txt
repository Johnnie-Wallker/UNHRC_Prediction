The successful candidates for the role of Special Rapporteur on contemporary forms of slavery, including its causes and consequences, within the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit a combination of legal expertise, international experience, and a strong academic background. Key features that distinguish these candidates include:

1. **Legal Expertise**: All shortlisted candidates possess a strong legal background, which is crucial for navigating the complexities of human rights law and effectively addressing issues related to slavery and its legal implications.

2. **International Experience**: Candidates have demonstrated experience in international governmental organizations (IGOs), non-governmental organizations (NGOs), or government roles, indicating their ability to operate within diverse international settings and engage with various stakeholders.

3. **Multilingual Proficiency**: While English proficiency is a common trait among all candidates, those with additional language skills, particularly French and Spanish, are favored due to the global nature of the mandate and the need for effective communication across different linguistic communities.

4. **Academic Credentials**: A significant number of candidates hold academic positions or have strong academic backgrounds, reflecting their deep understanding of theoretical and practical aspects of human rights issues, which is essential for conducting thorough research and formulating informed recommendations.

5. **Diverse Backgrounds**: The shortlisted candidates come from varied cultural and professional backgrounds, which enhances their ability to understand and address the multifaceted nature of slavery across different regions and communities.

These characteristics collectively ensure that the selected Special Rapporteur is well-equipped to investigate and report on contemporary forms of slavery, advocate for effective remedies, and collaborate with international bodies and civil society to combat this pervasive issue.
