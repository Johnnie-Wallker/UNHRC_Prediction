The successful candidates for the Special Rapporteur on the promotion and protection of the right to freedom of opinion and expression in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features include:

1. **Multilingual Proficiency**: Successful candidates demonstrate a high level of proficiency in English, which is essential for effective communication and advocacy within the international arena. Additionally, proficiency in other major languages such as French and Russian is advantageous, reflecting the global scope of the mandate.

2. **Diverse Professional Backgrounds**: Candidates with a robust mix of legal, academic, and practical experience in international, governmental, and non-governmental organizations are preferred. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of the legal frameworks, policy-making processes, and grassroots advocacy related to freedom of expression.

3. **Relevant Current Role**: Those currently engaged in roles that are law-related, academic, or involve significant interaction with international bodies or NGOs are seen as having the practical experience necessary to fulfill the responsibilities of the Special Rapporteur effectively.

4. **Geographical and Cultural Representation**: The inclusion of candidates from various regions, including Europe, the Americas, Africa, and Asia, ensures a balanced representation that can address the global challenges related to freedom of expression. This geographical diversity is crucial for understanding and responding to regional nuances and specificities.

5. **Age and Experience**: While age is not a definitive factor, a balance of seasoned professionals and those with fresh perspectives is beneficial. This mix can provide a blend of deep experience and innovative approaches to the mandate.

In summary, the ideal candidates for the Special Rapporteur position are those who possess a strong command of international languages, have varied and extensive professional backgrounds, are currently engaged in relevant roles, represent diverse geographical areas, and offer a mix of experience and fresh perspectives. These qualities collectively enhance the candidate's ability to advocate for and protect the right to freedom of opinion and expression on a global scale.
