The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (EMRIP) from Latin American and Caribbean States in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features include:

1. **Multilingual Proficiency**: Successful candidates demonstrate a strong command of multiple languages, particularly English and French, which are essential for effective communication and engagement within the international arena. Proficiency in Spanish is also beneficial given the region's linguistic context.

2. **Diverse Professional Backgrounds**: Candidates with a mix of legal, academic, NGO, government, and company backgrounds are favored. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of indigenous rights issues from various perspectives, including legal frameworks, practical implementation, and community engagement.

3. **Relevant Current Employment**: Those currently employed in roles related to international governmental organizations (IGOs), NGOs, or law are considered advantageous. This indicates active involvement and commitment to human rights and indigenous issues.

4. **Regional Representation**: Candidates from different countries within the Latin American and Caribbean region are preferred to ensure a broad representation of the area's diverse indigenous communities and issues.

5. **Age and Experience**: While age is not a definitive factor, candidates with a significant amount of experience, often reflected in their age, are likely to bring a depth of knowledge and maturity to the role.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the importance of language skills, diverse professional experiences, active engagement in relevant fields, and regional representation.
