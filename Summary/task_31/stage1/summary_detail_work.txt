The shortlisted candidates for the Working Group on Enforced or Involuntary Disappearances from Eastern European States in the UN Human Rights Council exhibit a robust blend of academic expertise, practical experience, and international advocacy in human rights, particularly in areas related to enforced disappearances, transitional justice, and international law.

All candidates possess a high level of proficiency in English, which is crucial for effective communication and collaboration within the international human rights community. Their backgrounds include significant roles in research, policy advising, and legal advocacy, reflecting a deep understanding of human rights issues and the mechanisms to address them.

One candidate, a dual citizen of Poland and Germany, has a strong academic track record as an assistant professor and postdoctoral researcher, focusing on human rights. Her experience includes part-time roles in renowned institutions, contributing to projects specifically on enforced disappearances.

Another candidate, a citizen of Albania, brings extensive experience as a member of the United Nations Committee on Migrant Workers and as a consultant on transitional justice and reconciliation. His background in international law and migration further complements his expertise in dealing with complex human rights issues.

The third candidate, a dual citizen of Slovakia and the United States, has a comprehensive career in legal advisory and advocacy, particularly in combating torture and other forms of violence. Her roles in international organizations and civil society have been pivotal in shaping legal standards and strategies for accountability in enforced disappearances.

Each candidate's diverse and complementary experiences make them well-suited to contribute effectively to the Working Group on Enforced or Involuntary Disappearances, enhancing the group's capacity to address and advocate for justice and rights protection in Eastern European States.
