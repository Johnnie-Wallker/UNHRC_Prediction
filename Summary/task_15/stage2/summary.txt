The successful candidates for the position of Special Rapporteur on the right of everyone to the enjoyment of the highest attainable standard of physical and mental health in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements:

1. **Diverse Backgrounds**: Successful candidates possess a blend of legal, academic, and government backgrounds. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of both the theoretical and practical aspects of human rights, particularly in the context of health standards.

2. **International Experience**: Candidates with experience in International Governmental Organizations (IGOs) and Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) are favored. This experience is crucial for navigating the complex international landscape of health rights and for effectively collaborating with various stakeholders.

3. **Language Proficiency**: While English proficiency is a must, candidates with additional language skills, particularly French and Spanish, are advantageous. This multilingual capability aids in broader communication and engagement with different global communities.

4. **Relevant Current Roles**: Successful candidates are currently engaged in roles that intersect academia, government, and international organizations. This indicates their active involvement and continued relevance in the field of human rights and health.

5. **Age and Experience**: The age range of successful candidates varies, suggesting that a balance of seasoned experience and fresh perspectives is valued. This mix ensures both deep knowledge and innovative approaches to addressing health rights issues.

6. **Cultural and Geographical Representation**: Candidates from diverse nationalities, including those from both developed and developing countries, are selected. This diversity enhances the ability to represent and understand the varied health rights challenges across different regions.

In summary, the ideal candidate for the Special Rapporteur position combines a robust legal and academic foundation with practical experience in international and governmental settings, possesses strong language skills to facilitate global communication, and represents a range of cultural and geographical perspectives.
