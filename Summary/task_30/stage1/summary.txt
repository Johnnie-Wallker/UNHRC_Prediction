The successful candidates for the Working Group of Experts on People of African Descent, member from Latin American and Caribbean States in the United Nations Human Rights Council, exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. 1. **Multilingual Proficiency**: Successful candidates demonstrated a strong command of English and Spanish, which are crucial for effective communication and collaboration within the international arena. Some also had a basic understanding of French, which adds to their linguistic versatility.

2. **Diverse Professional Backgrounds**: The shortlisted candidates had a mix of backgrounds including legal, academic, NGO, IGO, and government experience. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding and approach to human rights issues, leveraging different perspectives and expertise.

3. **Relevant Current Employment**: Candidates currently engaged in roles related to IGOs, NGOs, academia, or government positions were favored. This reflects their active involvement and commitment to human rights work, ensuring they bring current insights and practical experience to the group.

4. **Age Diversity**: The age range of the successful candidates varied, indicating a recognition of the value of experience alongside the energy and fresh perspectives that younger candidates can offer. This balance helps in addressing complex human rights issues with a wide-ranging approach.

5. **National Representation**: Candidates from various countries within the Latin American and Caribbean region were selected, highlighting the importance of regional representation to ensure the group's work is informed by a broad spectrum of local and regional experiences and challenges.

These key features underscore the importance of linguistic ability, professional diversity, active engagement in relevant fields, a balanced age profile, and regional representation in the selection process for the UNHRC.
