The successful candidates for the Special Rapporteur on the independence of judges and lawyers in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features include:

1. **Multilingual Proficiency**: Successful candidates demonstrate a high level of proficiency in English, which is essential for effective communication and documentation within the international arena. Additionally, many possess intermediate to high levels of proficiency in French and Spanish, languages that are widely spoken in various regions where the mandate may be applied.

2. **Diverse Professional Backgrounds**: Candidates with a mix of legal, academic, and international governmental organization (IGO), non-governmental organization (NGO), and government backgrounds are favored. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of both the theoretical and practical aspects of judicial independence and legal advocacy.

3. **Relevant Current Employment**: Those currently engaged in roles related to law, academia, IGOs, NGOs, or government positions are considered more suitable. This reflects their ongoing engagement with contemporary issues in judicial and legal systems, ensuring they are well-informed and active in the field.

4. **Geographical Representation**: While there is no strict geographical bias, candidates from various regions, including Europe, Latin America, and Africa, are represented among the successful applicants. This diversity helps in addressing global perspectives and challenges within the mandate.

5. **Age Diversity**: The age range of successful candidates varies, indicating a balance between experienced professionals and those with fresh perspectives. This mix is beneficial for bringing a wide range of experiences and innovative approaches to the role.

6. **Specific Expertise in Judicial Independence**: Candidates with a clear focus on legal issues, particularly those related to judicial independence and the rights of lawyers, are highlighted. This expertise is crucial for effectively addressing the mandate's objectives.

In summary, the selection process for the Special Rapporteur role emphasizes candidates who are multilingual, possess a blend of relevant professional experiences, are actively engaged in current legal or academic roles, represent diverse geographical areas, and have a strong focus on judicial independence and legal rights. These criteria ensure that the selected candidates are well-equipped to fulfill the mandate's objectives effectively.
