The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (EMRIP) from Africa in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features include:

1. **Strong Linguistic Proficiency**: Successful candidates typically demonstrate high levels of English proficiency, which is crucial for effective communication and participation in international forums. Some also possess high levels of French proficiency, reflecting the linguistic diversity of the African continent.

2. **Diverse Professional Backgrounds**: The candidates have varied and robust professional backgrounds that often include legal, academic, NGO, government, and international governmental organization (IGO) experience. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of indigenous rights issues from multiple perspectives.

3. **Relevant Current Job Experience**: Many successful candidates are currently engaged in roles that are directly or indirectly related to human rights, law, or advocacy. This includes positions in NGOs, IGOs, government bodies, and academic institutions, ensuring they are actively involved in the field of human rights.

4. **Geographical Representation**: The candidates come from a range of African countries, ensuring a broad representation of the continent's diverse indigenous populations. This geographical diversity is essential for addressing the specific needs and challenges faced by indigenous peoples across different regions.

5. **Age Diversity**: While there is no strict age criterion, the age range of successful candidates spans from the early 30s to late 60s, indicating a balance between experienced professionals and those with fresh perspectives.

6. **Expertise in Indigenous Rights**: The successful candidates often have a background or current involvement in areas directly related to indigenous rights, ensuring they possess the necessary expertise to contribute effectively to the EMRIP's objectives.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the importance of linguistic proficiency, diverse professional backgrounds, relevant current job experience, geographical representation, age diversity, and expertise in indigenous rights.
