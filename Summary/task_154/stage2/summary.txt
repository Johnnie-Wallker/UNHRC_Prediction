The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Right to Development (EMRTD) from African States in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features include:

1. **Multilingual Proficiency**: Successful candidates demonstrate a high level of proficiency in English, which is essential for effective communication and participation in international forums. Some candidates also possess additional language skills, such as French, which enhances their ability to engage with a broader range of stakeholders.

2. **Diverse Backgrounds**: Candidates with a mix of legal, academic, international governmental organization (IGO), non-governmental organization (NGO), and government backgrounds are favored. This diversity of experience ensures a comprehensive understanding of various aspects of human rights and development issues.

3. **Relevant Current Job Experience**: Candidates currently employed in roles related to law, academia, or NGOs are considered more suitable. This reflects their active engagement and practical experience in fields relevant to the mandate of the EMRTD.

4. **Geographical Representation**: Successful candidates come from various African countries, ensuring a balanced representation of the continent's diverse perspectives and experiences.

5. **Age Diversity**: The age range of successful candidates includes both younger and more experienced professionals, reflecting a blend of fresh perspectives and seasoned expertise.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the importance of language skills, diverse professional backgrounds, relevant job experience, geographical representation, and age diversity in identifying suitable candidates for the EMRTD within the UNHRC.
