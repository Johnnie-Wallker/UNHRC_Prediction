3. **Gender Balance**: The shortlisted candidates include both males and females, indicating a balanced representation of gender in the selection process. This suggests that the UNHRC values diversity in gender representation among its rapporteurs.

4. **Nationality and Regional Representation**: Candidates come from diverse national backgrounds, including the United Kingdom, United States, Iran, and Pakistan. 5. **Legal Tradition**: Candidates' success does not seem to be heavily influenced by a specific legal tradition. They have studied and worked in various legal systems, including common law and international law, indicating that a broad legal background is beneficial.

6. **Location of Education**: Successful candidates have studied in various locations, including both OECD countries and other regions. This suggests that the diversity of educational experiences, reflecting different legal and cultural contexts, is valued in preparing candidates to address global issues related to freedom of religion or belief.

In conclusion, the selection process for the Special Rapporteur on freedom of religion or belief emphasizes a diverse range of ages, language skills, gender, nationalities, legal traditions, and educational backgrounds. This diversity ensures that the rapporteur is well-equipped to address the complex and varied issues related to freedom of religion or belief across the globe.
