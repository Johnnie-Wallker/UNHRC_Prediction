The successful candidates for the Working Group on human rights and transnational corporations and other business enterprises from African States in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features include:

1. **Diverse Backgrounds**: Successful candidates possess a combination of legal, academic, and practical backgrounds. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of both theoretical and practical aspects of human rights issues related to transnational corporations.

2. **Multilingual Proficiency**: While English proficiency is a must, some candidates also demonstrate skills in other languages such as French, which is crucial for broader communication and engagement within the international community.

3. **Age Diversity**: The age range of successful candidates spans from the mid-30s to late 40s, indicating a balance between experienced professionals and those with fresh perspectives and innovative approaches.

4. **Relevant Current Roles**: Candidates currently engaged in academic, legal, and corporate roles are favored. This reflects their active involvement and practical experience in fields directly related to the mandate.

5. **International Exposure**: Some candidates have dual citizenship or experience working in multiple countries, which enhances their ability to navigate and address international human rights issues effectively.

6. **Government and NGO Experience**: Candidates with experience in government or NGOs are particularly valued as they bring insights into policy-making and grassroots human rights advocacy, respectively.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the need for well-rounded individuals with a strong foundation in legal and academic disciplines, practical experience, and the ability to communicate effectively in multiple languages.
