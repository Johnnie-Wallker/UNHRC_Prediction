The successful candidates for the Special Rapporteur on the independence of judges and lawyers in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features provide a clear guideline for the candidate selection process:

1. **Multilingual Proficiency**: Successful candidates demonstrate a high level of proficiency in English, which is essential for effective communication and report writing within the international context of the UNHRC. Additionally, some candidates possess high or intermediate proficiency in French, enhancing their ability to engage with a broader audience. Knowledge of Spanish, although not mandatory, is beneficial given the geographical focus of the mandate.

2. **Diverse Professional Backgrounds**: Candidates with a robust mix of legal, academic, and international governmental organization (IGO), non-governmental organization (NGO), and government backgrounds are favored. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of legal frameworks, practical implementation challenges, and advocacy strategies related to judicial independence and the rule of law.

3. **Relevant Current Employment**: Successful candidates are currently engaged in roles that are directly related to law, academia, or governance. This active involvement ensures that they are up-to-date with contemporary issues and developments in the field of judicial independence and legal practice.

4. **Age and Experience**: The age range of successful candidates typically spans from the late 30s to the early 70s, indicating a preference for candidates with a balance of youthful energy and seasoned experience. This combination is crucial for navigating complex international legal landscapes and for effectively representing the mandate.

5. **Geographical Representation**: While there is no strict geographical bias, candidates from diverse regions, including Europe, Latin America, and Asia, are represented among the successful applicants. This diversity is important for ensuring a global perspective and for addressing regional specificities within the mandate.

In summary, the selection process for the Special Rapporteur on the independence of judges and lawyers emphasizes candidates who are multilingual, possess a varied and relevant professional background, are actively involved in related fields, and represent a range of ages and geographical regions. These criteria ensure that the selected candidate is well-equipped to address the multifaceted challenges associated with the mandate.
