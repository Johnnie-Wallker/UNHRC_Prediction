The successful candidates for the Special Rapporteur on the human rights of internally displaced persons in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features provide a clear guideline for future candidate selection:

1. **Multifaceted Backgrounds**: Successful candidates possess a diverse range of professional backgrounds, including legal, academic, NGO, government, and international governmental organization (IGO) experiences. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of both the legal frameworks and practical challenges associated with human rights, particularly in the context of internally displaced persons.

2. **Strong Legal and Academic Foundations**: All shortlisted candidates have a solid legal background, indicating a deep understanding of human rights law and international legal standards. Additionally, many have academic backgrounds, which suggest a capacity for research, analysis, and the development of evidence-based recommendations.

3. **International Exposure**: Candidates with experience in IGOs and NGOs are favored, reflecting the need for global perspectives and the ability to navigate complex international dynamics. This exposure is crucial for addressing the cross-border implications of internal displacement.

4. **Government and Law-Related Experience**: The majority of successful candidates currently hold positions related to government and law, which is essential for influencing policy and advocating for the rights of internally displaced persons at a national and international level.

5. **Language Proficiency**: While English is a common requirement, candidates with additional language skills, particularly French and Spanish, are advantageous due to the global nature of the mandate and the need to communicate effectively with a diverse range of stakeholders.

6. **Age Diversity**: The shortlisted candidates span a range of ages, from mid-30s to 60s, indicating that both seasoned professionals and those with more recent, innovative perspectives are valued. This diversity ensures a balance of experience and fresh insights.

In summary, the selection criteria for the Special Rapporteur position emphasize candidates with a robust legal and academic foundation, diverse international experience, current involvement in government and law-related fields, and a proficiency in multiple languages. This combination of attributes ensures that the selected candidate is well-equipped to address the multifaceted challenges of protecting the human rights of internally displaced persons on a global scale.
