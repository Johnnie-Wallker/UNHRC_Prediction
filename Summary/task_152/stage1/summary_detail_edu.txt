The summary of key features of successful candidates for the Special Rapporteur on the human rights of internally displaced persons in the United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC) reveals several trends and preferences in the selection process:

1. **Age Range**: The successful candidates exhibit a broad age range, spanning from mid-30s to 60s. This indicates that experience and maturity are valued, but so is the energy and innovative thinking that younger candidates might bring.

2. **Language Abilities**: Fluency in English is a common requirement, with most candidates possessing high proficiency in this language. Additionally, there is a notable ability among some candidates to communicate in multiple languages, including French, Spanish, and Arabic, reflecting the global scope of the mandate and the need for rapporteurs to engage with diverse linguistic communities.

3. **Gender Balance**: The shortlisted candidates include both males and females, suggesting that the selection process does not overtly favor one gender over the other. The proportion of each gender among the successful candidates appears to be balanced, aligning with the UN's commitment to gender equality in leadership roles.

4. **Nationality and Regional Representation**: The successful candidates come from diverse national backgrounds, including countries from different regions such as Europe, North America, Asia, and Africa. This diversity reflects the global nature of human rights issues and the need for a broad perspective in addressing the challenges faced by internally displaced persons.

5. **Legal Tradition**: Candidates with a strong legal background, particularly in international law, are prevalent among the successful candidates. This suggests that a legal education grounded in international human rights law is advantageous for this mandate, as it equips candidates with the necessary expertise to navigate complex legal frameworks and advocate effectively for the rights of the displaced.

6. **Location of Education**: Successful candidates have received education in various locations, including both developed and developing countries. This indicates that the diversity of educational experiences is valued, allowing candidates to draw on a wide range of perspectives and methodologies in their work.

In conclusion, the selection process for the Special Rapporteur on the human rights of internally displaced persons emphasizes a balance of experience and innovation, linguistic proficiency, gender equality, and regional and educational diversity. Candidates with a robust legal background in international law, regardless of their specific national legal traditions, are particularly well-positioned to succeed in this role.
