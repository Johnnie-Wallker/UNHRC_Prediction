The successful candidates for the position of Special Rapporteur on the human rights of internally displaced persons in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features include:

1. **Multifaceted Background**: Successful candidates possess a diverse range of professional backgrounds, including legal, academic, NGO, government, and international governmental organization (IGO) experience. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding of the various dimensions of human rights issues related to internally displaced persons.

2. **Language Proficiency**: High proficiency in English is a common trait among successful candidates, reflecting the global language of the UN. Additionally, some candidates demonstrate proficiency in French, which is beneficial given the organization's operations in Francophone countries.

3. **Relevant Current Employment**: Candidates currently employed in roles related to law, academia, or IGOs are favored. These positions typically involve extensive engagement with human rights issues and international law, providing the necessary expertise to address the complexities of internal displacement.

4. **Age and Experience**: While age varies, successful candidates generally have a substantial amount of professional experience, ranging from mid-career to senior levels. This experience equips them with the necessary skills to navigate the political and humanitarian challenges associated with the mandate.

5. **International Perspective**: Candidates with a background in international operations, whether through IGOs, NGOs, or government roles, are preferred. This international exposure ensures a broad understanding of global human rights issues and the ability to work effectively across different cultural and political landscapes.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the need for candidates with a robust and diverse professional background, strong language skills, relevant current employment, significant experience, and an international perspective.
