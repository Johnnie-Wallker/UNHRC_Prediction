The successful candidates for the Special Rapporteur on the implications for human rights of the environmentally sound management and disposal of hazardous substances and wastes in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features provide a clear guideline for the candidate selection process:

1. **Multilingual Proficiency**: Successful candidates demonstrate proficiency in multiple languages, particularly English and French, which are essential for effective communication and collaboration within the international community. Some also possess additional language skills, enhancing their ability to engage with diverse stakeholders.

2. **Robust Legal Background**: A strong legal background is a common trait among successful candidates. This expertise is crucial for navigating complex legal frameworks and ensuring that environmental policies and practices are aligned with human rights standards.

3. **Diverse Professional Experience**: Candidates with a mix of academic, NGO, government, and international organization (IGO) backgrounds are favored. This diversity of experience allows them to understand and address environmental issues from various perspectives, ensuring comprehensive and effective solutions.

4. **Current Engagement in Relevant Fields**: Successful candidates are actively involved in roles that intersect with academia, law, and environmental issues. This ongoing engagement ensures that they are up-to-date with the latest developments and challenges in the field, making them well-equipped to fulfill the Special Rapporteur's duties.

5. **Geographical and Cultural Diversity**: Candidates from different countries and regions are selected, reflecting the global nature of environmental and human rights issues. This diversity ensures that the Special Rapporteur can represent and address concerns from various geographical contexts.

6. **Age and Experience**: While age is not a definitive factor, successful candidates typically have a significant amount of experience, which is beneficial for handling the complex and multifaceted responsibilities of the role.

By focusing on these key features, the UNHRC can ensure that the selected Special Rapporteur is well-prepared to address the mandate's objectives effectively and advocate for the protection of human rights in the context of environmental management and disposal of hazardous substances and wastes.
