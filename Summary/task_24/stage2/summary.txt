The successful candidates for the Special Rapporteur on the independence of judges and lawyers in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit several key features that align with the mandate's requirements. These features provide a clear guideline for the candidate selection process:

1. **Multifaceted Background**: Successful candidates typically possess a diverse background that includes experience in legal, academic, NGO, IGO, and government sectors. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding and approach to the issues related to judicial independence and the role of lawyers.

2. **Strong Legal and Academic Expertise**: A robust legal background is crucial, as it directly relates to the mandate's focus on judicial independence and legal practices. Additionally, academic expertise provides a foundation for research, analysis, and the development of informed recommendations.

3. **International Experience**: Candidates with backgrounds in international governmental organizations (IGOs) and non-governmental organizations (NGOs) are favored, reflecting the global nature of the mandate and the need for international collaboration and advocacy.

4. **Language Proficiency**: Proficiency in English is essential, given its widespread use in international forums. Knowledge of other languages, particularly French and Spanish, is advantageous due to their relevance in various regions and their use in international legal contexts.

5. **Relevant Current Role**: Candidates currently engaged in roles that are law-related or involve academic research are considered favorable, as these positions align with the investigative and analytical nature of the Special Rapporteur's duties.

6. **Geographical and Cultural Diversity**: Candidates from diverse geographical backgrounds are preferred, ensuring a broad perspective and understanding of issues related to judicial independence across different regions and legal systems.

These key features collectively ensure that the selected candidates are well-equipped to address the complex and multifaceted issues related to the independence of judges and lawyers, fulfilling the mandate's objectives within the UNHRC.
