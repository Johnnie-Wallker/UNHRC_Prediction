The summary of key features of successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Right to Development (EMRTD) in the UNHRC reveals several notable trends and characteristics:

5. **Legal Tradition**: The candidates' legal backgrounds are diverse, with education and experience in various legal traditions. This suggests that a broad understanding of different legal systems and international law is advantageous.

6. **Location of Education**: The diversity in the location of education is evident, with candidates having studied in both OECD countries and global south institutions. This indicates that a varied educational background, encompassing different legal and cultural contexts, is valued.

In conclusion, the selection criteria for the EMRTD in the UNHRC emphasize a balance of experience, language proficiency, gender diversity, and educational and cultural diversity. Candidates who demonstrate a broad understanding of international law and human rights, coupled with the ability to engage in multilingual and multicultural contexts, are more likely to be successful.
