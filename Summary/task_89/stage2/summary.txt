The successful candidates for the Expert Mechanism on the Right to Development (EMRTD) from Latin American and Caribbean States in the United Nations Human Rights Council exhibit a combination of strong linguistic abilities, diverse professional backgrounds, and relevant experience in international human rights. Key features of these candidates include:

1. **Bilingual or Trilingual Proficiency**: Successful candidates demonstrate proficiency in English and French, which are essential for effective communication and negotiation within the international arena. Some also possess intermediate Spanish skills, which is beneficial given the regional context.

2. **Diverse Professional Backgrounds**: Candidates have a mix of legal, academic, international governmental organization (IGO), non-governmental organization (NGO), and government backgrounds. This diversity ensures a comprehensive understanding and approach to the complexities of human rights issues, particularly in the context of development.

3. **Relevant Experience**: Candidates with experience in IGOs, NGOs, and academia are particularly valued, as they bring a wealth of knowledge and practical experience in advocating for and implementing human rights policies and initiatives.

4. **Age and Cultural Diversity**: The age range of the candidates, spanning from the mid-40s to the early 60s, indicates a balance between seasoned expertise and continued vigor in contributing to human rights work. Additionally, candidates with dual citizenship reflect a broader cultural understanding and sensitivity to regional issues.

5. **Current Relevant Employment**: Successful candidates are currently engaged in roles that are directly related to human rights and development, such as academic positions or work within IGOs. This ongoing engagement ensures that they are up-to-date with current trends and challenges in the field.

These key features provide a clear guideline for the selection process, emphasizing the importance of linguistic competence, diverse and relevant professional backgrounds, and active engagement in human rights and development work.
