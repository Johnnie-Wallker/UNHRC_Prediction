The summary of the key features of successful candidates for the Special Rapporteur on violence against women, its causes and consequences in the UNHRC reveals several trends and characteristics that are indicative of the selection criteria:

1. **Age Range**: The successful candidates tend to be in the older age bracket, with most candidates being over 50 years old. This suggests that extensive experience and a mature perspective are valued for this mandate.

2. **Language Abilities**: Fluency in English is a common requirement, with many candidates also possessing proficiency in French and/or Arabic. This indicates that language skills in major international languages are important for effective communication and understanding across diverse regions.

3. **Gender**: The mandate focuses on women's rights, and thus, it is predominantly women who are shortlisted. This reflects a deliberate effort to ensure that the rapporteur is aligned with the subject matter of the mandate.

4. **Nationality and Region**: Candidates from diverse regions are represented, with a notable presence from countries in the Middle East, North Africa, South Asia, and North America. This diversity ensures a broad understanding of the issues related to violence against women across different cultural and socio-political contexts.

5. **Legal Tradition**: Candidates with a strong background in international human rights law, particularly those educated in or from countries with well-established legal traditions in human rights, are more likely to be successful. This suggests that familiarity with international legal frameworks and mechanisms is crucial.

6. **Location of Education**: Education from a variety of locations, including both OECD countries and the global south, is represented among the successful candidates. This diversity in educational backgrounds suggests that a broad understanding of human rights issues from different global perspectives is valued.

In conclusion, the selection process for the Special Rapporteur on violence against women emphasizes candidates who are mature, possess strong language skills in major international languages, are predominantly female, come from diverse national and regional backgrounds, have a solid grounding in international human rights law, and have received education from a variety of global locations. These criteria ensure that the rapporteur is well-equipped to address the complex and sensitive issues related to violence against women on an international scale.
